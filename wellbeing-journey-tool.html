Support for Teens & Parents During Social Media Changes

With new social media restrictions for under-16s in Australia, many teens are feeling anxious, restless, or unsure what to do with themselves. This page is here to help teens calm their nervous system — and help parents support them without pressure or panic.

For Teens: “This Feels Big — And That’s Okay”

If your phone suddenly feels quieter, your brain might panic. That doesn’t mean something is wrong with you. It means your nervous system is adjusting.

You’re allowed to feel bored. You’re allowed to feel annoyed. And you’re allowed to discover new parts of yourself offline.

60-Second Calm-Down
  • Put one hand on your chest
  • Breathe in through your nose for 4 seconds
  • Breathe out through your mouth for 6 seconds
  • Repeat 3 times

This tells your body: “I am safe right now.”

For Parents: What Helps Most

Helpful things to say:

  • “I get why this feels upsetting.”
  • “You’re not in trouble for feeling like this.”
  • “Let’s find something that helps you calm down.”

Try to avoid:

  • “You’ll get over it.”
  • “Just be grateful.”
  • “Other kids have it worse.”

Teens don’t need fixing first — they need regulating first.

Why Journaling Helps Teens

Journaling helps teens slow racing thoughts, release anxiety safely, and build emotional confidence. Even 5 minutes a day can help the nervous system settle.

  • “Right now I feel…”
  • “The hardest part today was…”
  • “One thing I handled better than I thought…”
